Master Legal Services Agreement & Retainer

Webull Brand Impersonation & Digital Asset Recovery

Effective Date:

THIS AGREEMENT (the "Agreement") is made and entered into as of the date of electronic execution (the "Effective Date") by and between:

LEGAL COUNSEL: THE CRYPTO LAWYERS PLLC, a professional limited liability company organized under the laws of the State of TEXAS, with a principal business address of 1005 Congress Avenue, Suite 925, Austin, Texas 78701 (hereinafter referred to as the "Firm").

CLIENT: The individual executing this Agreement electronically via digital signature, whose identity and mailing address are recorded in the associated Case Intake File (hereinafter referred to as the "Client").

The Firm and the Client may be referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

1. Recitals

WHEREAS the Client alleges to have suffered financial loss due to fraud, negligence, or misappropriation of digital assets involving Webull Corporation and,

WHEREAS the Client desires to retain the Firm to provide legal representation to pursue recovery of said assets, plus compensatory damages, statutory penalties, and reparations for harm caused, where legally viable,

WHEREAS the Firm agrees to represent the Client subject to the terms, conditions, and strictly limited scope defined herein,

NOW, THEREFORE in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:

2. Scope of Services

2.1 Investigation: Forensic analysis of blockchain transactions to establish a nexus of liability.

2.2 Pre-Litigation Actions: Drafting and serving formal Demand Letters, Cease and Desist Notices, and preservation of evidence requests.

2.3 Dispute Resolution: Representation of the Client in Individual Arbitration proceedings or settlement negotiations.

2.4 Exclusion of Class Action: The Client explicitly acknowledges that this engagement is for an INDIVIDUAL CLAIM ONLY. The Client knowingly opts out of any potential Class Action lawsuit to pursue this matter individually.

2.5 No Guarantee of Outcome: The Firm will exercise due diligence in representing the Client; however, the Firm does not guarantee any specific result, recovery amount, or outcome.

3. Compensation and Fees

In consideration for the Services, the Client agrees to the following "Hybrid" fee structure:

3.1 Initial Retainer (Flat Fee): The Client shall pay the Retainer. This fee is earned upon receipt and covers the immediate costs of case intake, forensic auditing, and administrative setup.

3.2 Contingency Fee (Success Fee): In the event of a recovery, the Firm shall be entitled to Fifteen Percent (15%) of the Gross Recovery.

"Gross Recovery" is defined as the total value of all cash, cryptocurrency, or property received by the Client from the Target Entity or any third party in connection with this claim.

3.3 Attorneys' Lien: The Client hereby grants the Firm a contractual and common law lien upon the Client's claim and any proceeds recovered, to the full extent of the Fees and Costs due under this Agreement.

3.4 Costs and Expenses: The Client shall be responsible for all costs and expenses incurred in connection with the Services, including but not limited to filing fees, court costs, expert witness fees, and travel expenses. Such costs shall be deducted from any recovery prior to distribution to the Client.

3.5 Fee Disputes: Any dispute regarding fees or costs shall be resolved through the Texas fee arbitration process as provided by the State Bar of Texas.

4. Payment Mechanics (Blockchain Policy)

4.1 Digital Assets:

Crypto Payments

  • USDT on TRON (TRC-20)
  • USDC on Ethereum (ERC-20)
  • Bitcoin (Native SegWit)

Bank Wire/ACH

  • Available for cases with Reported Loss ≥ $100,000 USD
  • Bank details provided post-signature

(a) Retainer Payment: The Client shall remit the Initial Retainer via the methods listed above.

(b) Settlement Wallet Designation: The wallet address or bank account used to remit the Initial Retainer shall automatically serve as the designated receiving address for all recovered principal, damages, reparations, and other consideration ("Settlement Wallet"). Client confirms they control this Settlement Wallet and authorize the Firm to distribute recovered funds thereto.

(c) Digital Asset Terms: For crypto payments, USD value is locked at blockchain confirmation time via public market data (CoinGecko). Client bears volatility risk between agreement signing and payment confirmation. The Firm will convert crypto retainers to fiat or stablecoin within 48 hours per Texas IOLTA rules.

(d) Network Warning: Sending assets to an incompatible network (e.g., ERC-20 tokens to TRC-20 address) will result in permanent loss of funds. Recovery is not guaranteed.

4.2 Network Fees: The Client assumes full responsibility for any blockchain network fees ("Gas Fees") associated with the transfer of the Retainer or Settlement funds.

4.3 Rationale for Cryptocurrency Acceptance: The Firm accepts cryptocurrency as a form of payment for the following reasons:

(a) Client Demographics: The Firm's clients are victims of cryptocurrency-related fraud who predominantly operate within the digital asset ecosystem. Accepting cryptocurrency eliminates unnecessary friction and enables clients to retain capital within their existing financial infrastructure.

(b) Asset Preservation: Many Clients have liquidated traditional assets to fund cryptocurrency investments and subsequently lost those funds to fraud. Cryptocurrency acceptance provides a familiar and accessible payment mechanism without requiring Clients to re-establish traditional banking relationships that may have been disrupted.

(c) Operational Efficiency: Cryptocurrency transactions settle within minutes rather than the multi-day processing times associated with international wire transfers, enabling the Firm to commence forensic analysis and legal proceedings without delay.

(d) Regulatory Compliance: All cryptocurrency payments are converted to United States Dollars (USD) within forty-eight (48) hours of blockchain confirmation in accordance with Texas IOLTA rules and State Bar of Texas regulations governing client trust accounts.

(e) Transparency: All cryptocurrency transactions are recorded on public blockchains, providing an immutable audit trail of payment for both the Client and the Firm.

5. Term and Termination

5.1 Term: This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue until the matter is resolved via settlement, judgment, or denial, or until terminated as provided herein.

5.2 Termination by Client: The Client may terminate this Agreement at any time upon written notice to the Firm. In such event, the Client shall be responsible for all fees and costs incurred up to the date of termination, including any work performed on a contingency basis.

5.3 Termination by Firm: The Firm may terminate this Agreement upon written notice to the Client if: (a) the Client fails to pay fees or costs when due; (b) the Client fails to cooperate or provide requested information; (c) the Client insists upon action that is contrary to the Firm's advice; or (d) the Firm determines that a conflict of interest exists.

5.4 Effect of Termination: Upon termination, the Firm shall: (a) refund any unearned portion of the retainer; (b) provide the Client with copies of all documents in the Client's file; and (c) retain a lien for any unpaid fees or costs as described in Section 3.3.

6. Relationship of Parties (Independent Contractor)

The Firm is an independent contractor and not an employee of the Client.

6.1 Control: The Firm has the sole right to control and direct the means, manner, and method by which the Services are performed.

6.2 Non-Exclusivity: The Firm retains the right to perform legal services for other clients during the term of this Agreement, provided no direct conflict of interest exists.

6.3 No Agency: Neither Party has the authority to bind the other Party to any contract or obligation outside the scope of this Agreement.

7. Confidentiality and Privilege

7.1 Attorney-Client Privilege: Communications between the Client and the Firm are protected by Attorney-Client Privilege to the fullest extent of the law.

7.2 Proprietary Information: The Client acknowledges that the Firm's legal strategies, templates, forensic methods, and negotiation techniques constitute "Trade Secrets." The Client agrees not to disclose, share, or publish the Firm's work product to third parties without written consent.

7.3 Data Privacy: The Firm shall take reasonable steps to protect the Client's personal data and financial information in compliance with standard legal ethics.

8. Return of Records and File Retention

Upon termination of this Agreement, and upon the Client's request, the Firm shall provide the Client with copies of all documents in the Client's file. The Firm may retain copies for its records as permitted by applicable law.

8.1 File Retention: The Firm shall retain the Client's file for a period of seven (7) years from the conclusion of the matter, after which the file may be destroyed without further notice to the Client.

9. Dispute Resolution and Arbitration

9.1 Mandatory Arbitration: Any dispute, claim, or controversy arising out of or relating to this Agreement or the breach, termination, enforcement, interpretation, or validity thereof, shall be determined by final and binding arbitration in Austin, Texas, before a single arbitrator administered by the American Arbitration Association ("AAA") under its Consumer Arbitration Rules.

9.2 Arbitration Procedures: The arbitration shall be conducted in English. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding, and judgment on the award rendered by the arbitrator may be entered in any court having jurisdiction thereof.

9.3 Costs of Arbitration: Each Party shall bear its own costs and attorneys' fees in any arbitration proceeding, unless the arbitrator determines otherwise.

9.4 Class Action Waiver: The Parties agree that any arbitration shall be conducted on an individual basis only and not in a class, consolidated, or representative action.

9.5 Injunctive Relief: Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may seek injunctive or other equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to prevent the actual or threatened infringement, misappropriation, or violation of intellectual property rights or confidential information.

10. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

10.1 Governing Law: This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Texas, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

10.2 Jurisdiction: Subject to the arbitration provisions in Section 9, the Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in Austin, Texas, for any action or proceeding arising out of this Agreement.

11. Indemnification

11.1 Client Indemnification: The Client agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Firm, its partners, associates, employees, and agents from and against any and all claims, liabilities, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to: (a) the Client's breach of this Agreement; (b) any misrepresentation or omission by the Client; or (c) any third-party claim related to the Client's underlying dispute.

11.2 Firm Indemnification: The Firm agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Client from and against any and all claims, liabilities, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to the Firm's gross negligence or willful misconduct in the performance of the Services.

12. Limitation of Liability

12.1 Limitation: TO THE MAXIMUM EXTENT PERMITTED BY APPLICABLE LAW, THE FIRM'S TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE AMOUNT OF FEES ACTUALLY PAID BY THE CLIENT TO THE FIRM UNDER THIS AGREEMENT DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE LIABILITY.

12.2 No Consequential Damages: IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO LOSS OF PROFITS, DATA, OR BUSINESS OPPORTUNITIES, ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT.

13. Force Majeure

Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performing its obligations under this Agreement to the extent such failure or delay results from circumstances beyond the reasonable control of such Party, including but not limited to: acts of God, natural disasters, pandemic, government actions, war, terrorism, cyberattacks, blockchain network failures, or internet outages.

14. Assignment

The Client may not assign or transfer this Agreement or any rights or obligations hereunder without the prior written consent of the Firm. The Firm may assign this Agreement to an affiliated entity or successor in interest without the Client's consent.

15. Notices

All notices required or permitted under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed delivered when: (a) delivered personally; (b) sent by email to the address on file; or (c) deposited with a nationally recognized overnight courier. Notices to the Firm shall be sent to the address listed above. Notices to the Client shall be sent to the email or mailing address in the Case Intake File.

16. Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. The invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable while preserving the Parties' original intent.

17. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions, whether oral or written. No modification or amendment of this Agreement shall be valid unless in writing and signed by both Parties.

18. Waiver

The failure of either Party to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of such provision or the right to enforce it at a later time. Any waiver must be in writing and signed by the waiving Party.

19. Survival

The provisions of Sections 3 (Compensation and Fees), 7 (Confidentiality and Privilege), 9 (Dispute Resolution and Arbitration), 10 (Governing Law and Jurisdiction), 11 (Indemnification), and 12 (Limitation of Liability) shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement.
